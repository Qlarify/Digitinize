How to audit your B2B pipeline before hiring any agency.

Most B2B agency engagements fail in the first 90 days because the audit happens after the contract is signed. Here's the 5-dimension framework we run with every diagnostic client — so you can run it on yourself first.

By Zeeshan Soudagar · Founder, Digitinize · Published 14 May 2026

TL;DR

  • Most B2B agency engagements fail in the first 90 days because the audit happens after the contract is signed.
  • Score five dimensions before you brief anyone: pipeline shape, ICP clarity, channel attribution, content inventory, operational readiness.
  • A printable scorecard at the end. Your /25 score tells you whether you need an agency, a consultant, or an interim CMO.

Every quarter, a marketing head reaches out to us with the same story. They've been pitched by four agencies. Three sent generic decks. One actually asked a question. They're confused about who to pick — and they're under board pressure to "fix marketing."

Here's the uncomfortable truth: none of those agencies can give you a real answer until they understand what's broken. And until you understand what's broken, you can't tell which agency is bluffing.

The audit framework below is what we run in our own diagnostic phase. We're publishing it because it solves a problem we see weekly: B2B teams hiring agencies on top of a pipeline they don't fully understand, then blaming the agency when results don't land.

If you do this audit before you brief anyone, three things happen. You stop being a price-shopping buyer. You can tell within ten minutes whether an agency is going to ask the right questions. And you have a baseline to measure improvement against — not the agency's vanity dashboard.

This will take you 4–6 hours across two days. It is worth more than every demo call you'll take this month.

Dimension 1: Pipeline shape

Before any agency conversation, you should be able to answer four questions cold.

Where does pipeline come from today?

Pull the last four full quarters. For every closed-won deal, tag the first-touch source (how they first encountered you) and the last-touch source (what was attributed in CRM). The gap between the two is your attribution debt — and it's usually 40–60% wider than people think.

If your CRM doesn't capture first-touch reliably, that's not a marketing problem yet — it's a tracking problem. Fix it before you hire anyone, or every agency will optimize for whatever your CRM happens to attribute (usually last-touch paid or "direct").

What's pipeline velocity by stage?

Stage 1 → 2, 2 → 3, all the way to closed-won. Where do deals stall? If most pipeline dies in stage 2 (post-demo), no top-of-funnel agency will fix it — you have a sales enablement or product-market-fit problem. If pipeline never reaches stage 2, you have a top-of-funnel problem an agency can address.

What's conversion at each stage?

Indicative B2B SaaS medians: MQL → SQL around 13%, SQL → Opportunity around 6%, Opportunity → Closed-Won 15–22%. Your numbers don't need to match these — they need to be known.

What's your CAC trend?

Not just CAC — CAC over the last 12 months, segmented by channel. If your blended CAC is rising and you don't know which channel is dragging it up, an agency that promises "lower CAC" is selling you a horoscope.

Score this dimension 0–5:

  • 0: You don't have any of this data
  • 2: You have closed-won numbers but no stage-by-stage funnel
  • 3: You have the funnel but not segmented by channel
  • 4: You have it segmented, but only as of this quarter
  • 5: You have 4+ quarters of segmented funnel data

We've never worked with a Score 0 client where an agency was the right next step. Hire a RevOps consultant first.

Dimension 2: ICP clarity

Open your closed-won list from the last 12 months. Now open the ICP your marketing team is targeting. Compare them.

If you can't find your stated ICP in your top 20 closed-won deals, your marketing is not aligned to your business. This is the most common gap we see — and the most expensive to fix retroactively.

Ask these questions:

Who actually pays you?

Sort closed-won by ACV. The top 20% of customers usually drive 60–80% of revenue. What do they have in common? Industry, company size, role of buyer, stage of growth, geography, tech stack, trigger event. List five attributes.

Who closes fastest?

Sort by sales-cycle length. The fastest-closing segment is your demand-side ICP. The biggest-ACV segment is your value-side ICP. These are often different people. Most agencies will optimize for whichever you say first.

Who churns?

If you're a SaaS or recurring-revenue business, look at the segment that churns inside 12 months. That's not your ICP — it's a leak. Stop marketing to lookalikes of churned accounts. Sounds obvious. Most teams don't.

Who do sales reps say "yes" to immediately?

Walk over to your top AE. Ask: "When you see a lead come in, what makes you actually pick up the phone?" That answer is the ICP — not the persona doc the marketing team built in 2023.

Score 0–5:

  • 0: No documented ICP, or one that doesn't match closed-won
  • 3: Documented ICP, partially matches closed-won
  • 5: ICP is documented, validated against closed-won, and segmented into "easiest to close" vs. "most valuable"

If you score below 3 here, the next 90 days of agency spend will be optimized for the wrong audience.

Dimension 3: Channel attribution

This is where most B2B teams flatter themselves.

Pull your marketing spend by channel for the last 12 months. Pull pipeline sourced from each channel. Calculate cost-per-pipeline-dollar — not cost-per-MQL. That metric has killed more B2B marketing teams than it's helped.

Two patterns repeat:

Pattern A: LinkedIn paid is over-credited

It's measurable, the dashboards look great, and most agencies pitch it because it's billable hours. But the share of pipeline LinkedIn paid actually originates (vs. touches) is usually 10–25% of what last-touch attribution suggests. Run a holdout test, or look at first-touch.

Pattern B: Organic is under-credited

Organic search, organic LinkedIn, and word-of-mouth referrals are usually 2–3× larger drivers of pipeline than CRM attribution shows, because the first touch happens 9–14 months before the deal closes and falls outside the attribution window.

Honest channel scorecard: for each channel, mark whether it's a driver (originates pipeline), an amplifier (touches pipeline already in flight), or vanity (looks busy, drives nothing). Most B2B teams have 2 drivers, 2 amplifiers, and 3 vanity channels. They're spending equally across all seven.

Before you hire an agency, decide which channel mix you want to build toward, not just optimize what exists.

Score 0–5:

  • 0: All decisions made on last-touch attribution alone
  • 2: Some sense of first-touch but no integration
  • 3: Multi-touch attribution exists but isn't used in budget decisions
  • 5: Channels classified as drivers/amplifiers/vanity; budget allocated accordingly

Dimension 4: Content & SEO inventory

This one is mechanical. Do it in a spreadsheet.

List every piece of content published in the last 24 months. For each:

  • Organic traffic last 90 days
  • Conversions or pipeline attributed (any window)
  • Last updated date
  • Author

You'll find a clear pattern. Roughly 20% of content drives 80% of organic traffic. Most of that 20% was published 6–18 months ago and hasn't been touched since. The other 80% is dead — published, indexed, and forgotten.

Before you brief any SEO or content agency, know:

  • What ranks today and what ranking trends look like (use Search Console, not third-party estimates, for clicks)
  • Which pages convert and which don't
  • Which pillar topics are owned by competitors that you should compete on
  • Which pillar topics are open

Most agencies that pitch SEO will tell you they'll "do an audit" in month one. Most of that audit is recoverable yourself in two hours if your Search Console is wired up. Do it. The agency conversation gets much sharper when you arrive with a content inventory and a ranking trend.

Score 0–5:

  • 0: No content inventory, no Search Console access
  • 3: Inventory exists but isn't analyzed against pipeline
  • 5: Inventory is segmented (winners/losers/dead), tied to pipeline, and updated quarterly

Dimension 5: Operational readiness

An agency can't fix a leaking funnel from the top. Check these before you hire:

CRM hygiene

Open ten random opportunity records. How many have populated source, stage, expected close date, and last-activity date? If fewer than seven, your CRM is hallucinating, and any agency reporting will inherit that hallucination.

Sales-marketing SLA

Is there a documented SLA on lead response time? Lead qualification criteria? Lead recycling? If not, your marketing team will pass leads sales never works, and you'll blame the agency for "low-quality leads."

Attribution stack

What touches are tracked? UTM hygiene? Form-to-CRM sync? Closed-loop reporting? If your stack is GA4 + a CRM + nothing else, you have last-touch attribution. Plan accordingly.

Editorial production capacity

If you're hiring a content agency, who reviews and approves work internally? If the answer is "the marketing head, between other things," your content production will bottleneck at one piece per month regardless of agency capacity. Either name a reviewer or accept a smaller scope.

Budget cadence

Is marketing budget committed quarterly, half-yearly, or annually? Agencies that promise compounding results in B2B (us included) need six-plus months. If your CFO will pull budget mid-quarter, say so up front and pick an agency model that survives it.

Score 0–5:

  • 0: None of the above documented
  • 3: CRM and attribution are fine, but no SLA or editorial process
  • 5: All five operationally clean

The scorecard

Total your five dimensions:

ScoreReading
0–8You don't need an agency yet. Hire a fractional CMO or RevOps consultant for 90 days first.
9–14You're agency-ready, but only for a diagnostic engagement. Don't sign a 12-month retainer until the diagnostic is complete.
15–20You're ready for a full retainer. Brief 2–3 agencies, not 7.
21–25You may not need an agency. You may need senior specialist hires inside your team.

What to do with this audit before you brief anyone

Don't share the scorecard with an agency. Share the inputs — the pipeline data, the ICP segmentation, the content inventory, the operational status. Watch what they do with it.

Good agencies will read it, ask six to eight sharp follow-up questions, and propose a diagnostic before a retainer. Bad agencies will skip past the data and present a generic "growth roadmap" deck.

Brief template:

  1. Here's our pipeline shape (last 4 quarters)
  2. Here's our ICP and our current-quarter target segment
  3. Here's our channel mix and what we believe is working
  4. Here's our content inventory with ranking and conversion data
  5. Here's where we're stuck — be specific (e.g., "demos converting at 8% to opportunity, half our benchmark")
  6. Tell us what you'd diagnose first

Any agency that can't engage with this brief on their first call should be removed from consideration.

Closing

Hiring an agency is a 6–18 month commitment. The cost of the wrong agency is rarely the fees — it's the opportunity cost of a year of misaligned execution. The audit above takes 4–6 hours. The downstream cost of skipping it is twelve months and several crore.
