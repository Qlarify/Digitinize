The problem with agencies

You're not short on agencies.
You're short on accountability.

  • You hired an expert on the call, but an account manager shows up on day one
  • Strategy built from a template, not your category, your cycle, your unit economics
  • The team rotates every quarter; you re-brief from zero every time
  • Reports full of impressions and MQLs, but no one owns the pipeline number
  • When performance drops, the agency has seventeen reasons why. None of them theirs.
How we're different

We don't manage campaigns. We run your revenue marketing.

Digitinize operates as an embedded extension of your team: in your Slack, your standups, your strategy calls. We own the channels, the strategy, and the reporting. And we measure ourselves against one thing: pipeline.

  • Embedded, not external: inside your team structure, not above it
  • Accountable to pipeline: not traffic, not MQLs, not impressions
  • Senior operators: the people who sold you are the people who work on your account
  • Systems that compound: infrastructure that gets more efficient over time
  • Long-term by design: 18-month average engagement, not a 90-day sprint

How we engage

From first call to active pipeline.
In four stages.

01

The Diagnostic

Before we run anything, we understand everything. ICP, funnel performance, channel mix, tech stack, competitive position. Most agencies skip this. We treat it as the most valuable work we do together.

02

The Revenue Blueprint

A 90-day execution plan: channel priorities, campaign structures, content calendar, and success metrics. Not a strategy doc that sits in Notion. An operating plan.

03

Build & Activate

Paid campaigns live. Content pipeline running. Automation flows configured. Sales enablement assets ready. Everything activates on a tight, sequenced timeline.

04

Compound

Month over month: optimise what's working, cut what isn't, expand what's compounding. The goal is channels that get more efficient and more effective as data matures.
