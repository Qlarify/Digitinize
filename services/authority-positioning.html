04 / Authority Positioning & Executive Social

LinkedIn ghostwriting and authority positioning for founders and executives, India.

Many companies — B2B SaaS and IT firms, but also considered-purchase B2C brands in fintech, education, and premium services — have a positioning problem before they have a marketing problem. They sound like five competitors, their executives are invisible on LinkedIn, and their content reads like everyone else's. Our LinkedIn ghostwriting and authority positioning service — based in Bangalore — is the work of making your point of view sharper than your category, and distributing it where buyers actually pay attention.

The Problem

Why most authority positioning programs underperform.

They confuse volume with authority. Posting three times a week on LinkedIn isn't a positioning strategy — it's output without strategy. Volume without substance earns followers, not pipeline. Real authority is built on a small number of strongly-held opinions, defended in public, over a long period.

We design the opinions, build the editorial operating system, and run the distribution. The result: a founder who publishes less, says more, and generates pipeline from content — not just followers.

Why founder-led content wins

Personal brand outperforms company brand for considered purchases. Here's why.

LinkedIn's algorithm ranks personal profiles 6–8x higher than company pages for the same content. More importantly, buyers trust people before they trust organisations. When your ICP sees your company page, they see a vendor. When they see your founder, they see a peer.

📊

The LinkedIn reach gap

A company page post with 500 followers gets 80–120 impressions on average. A founder post with 2,000 followers gets 4,000–8,000 impressions — and that's before the algorithm distributes it to second-degree connections who engage. The same content reaches 30–60x more people through a personal profile.

🤝

Trust transfers from person to company

Research consistently shows that B2B buyers — and high-ticket B2C buyers in financial, education, and premium service categories — make vendor decisions based on perceived expertise of the team, not the company's messaging. A founder who is visibly authoritative in their category transfers that credibility to their firm. The company brand follows the personal brand, not the other way around.

🧲

Inbound that doesn't decay

A well-positioned founder generates inbound conversations that continue between posts. Buyers who follow a founder for 6–12 months develop a relationship before the first sales conversation — meaning deals start warmer, close faster, and require less discounting. Average sales cycle reduction in accounts sourced through founder content: 30–40%.

🏆

Category ownership

The first firm to plant a strong point of view in a category tends to own it — even if it isn't the largest. Buyers who've been consuming a founder's content for months will consider that firm first when they go in-market. Category ownership is not built with advertising. It's built with consistent, opinionated publishing.

The ghostwriting process

What our founder ghostwriting engagement actually looks like.

Founders often worry that ghostwritten content won't sound like them. In our experience, the opposite happens — professional ghostwriting makes founders sound more like themselves than their own first drafts. Here's the process.

01

Positioning workshop (Month 1 only)

A 2-hour structured interview covering: what you believe that most people in your category get wrong, what problems your buyers actually have vs. what they think they have, and what you'd say if you weren't worried about offending anyone. This surfaces the raw material for 12+ months of content.

02

Monthly interview (90 minutes)

Every month, one conversation. We ask; you talk. We record and transcribe. You don't need to prepare talking points — we run the conversation. From 90 minutes of unscripted thinking, we extract 4–6 LinkedIn posts, one long-form piece, and 2–3 short-form audio or video hooks.

03

Draft, edit, publish

We write first drafts. You get a 48-hour review window — typically 15–20 minutes of reading and light edits. We publish or you copy-paste and post. No more staring at a blank LinkedIn composer at 8am wondering what to write.

04

Quarterly position review

Every 90 days we review: which posts got traction, which topics your ICP engaged with most, what the category conversation has shifted to. We adjust the content strategy accordingly. Your positioning evolves as your market does.

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

What is LinkedIn ghostwriting?

LinkedIn ghostwriting is when a professional writer produces LinkedIn content — posts, articles, carousels — on behalf of an executive or founder. The executive's name and voice stay front and centre; the ghostwriter handles research, structure, drafting, and editing. The result is consistent, high-quality publishing without the founder spending 5–8 hours a week writing.

How much does LinkedIn ghostwriting cost in India?

Engagements typically start at ₹1.5L–₹2.5L per month for a single executive — covering positioning strategy, 8–10 posts per month, editorial calendar, and quarterly positioning reviews. The range depends on publishing frequency, number of executives, and whether the engagement includes LinkedIn ads coordination.

Do you ghostwrite for startup founders and CEOs specifically?

Yes — most of our authority positioning work is with founders and CEOs of B2B companies and considered-purchase B2C brands between ₹5Cr and ₹200Cr ARR. The sweet spot is a founder who has strong opinions and domain depth but no time or process to publish consistently. We extract the thinking through monthly interviews and turn it into a publishing system.

Do you actually ghostwrite our content?

Yes. Most founders don't have time to write twice a week. We work from interview transcripts and your edits — your voice, our production.

Will this work if our founder isn't already on LinkedIn?

Yes — starting point matters less than people assume. A focused point-of-view and consistent publishing builds a relevant audience over time. Founders with no existing presence often compound faster because there are no legacy audience expectations to manage.

How long until authority positioning shows up in pipeline?

Indirect impact at 90 days — engagement, profile visits, DMs. Direct impact — named referrals, inbound from content — at 6–9 months.

Can you do this for multiple executives, not just the founder?

Yes — multi-executive authority programs work well at companies above 50 employees. We sequence so positions don't overlap and each executive owns a distinct category conversation.

Is this just PR?

No. PR is third-party validation — coverage in publications. Authority positioning is first-party point-of-view — your founder's published opinions. They complement, but they're not the same.

We tried LinkedIn before and it didn't work — why would this be different?

Usually because it was output without strategy: posting for the sake of it, no defined positioning, no editorial system. We start with what you actually believe, build a content architecture around it, and tie every piece to a business outcome. Volume alone doesn't build authority. Point of view does.

How do you maintain confidentiality? Our competitors follow our founder on LinkedIn.

Strategically, that's fine — your competitors don't know what you plan to say next, and they're not your audience anyway. On confidentiality: we work under NDA. We never discuss client engagements, never reference your company in our own content, and use a separate team for each client to avoid cross-pollination of positioning. Everything stays inside a shared doc environment only you and the assigned team can access.

Our founder is a subject-matter expert but hates writing. Does that matter?

It actually helps. Founders who love writing often have the hardest time with ghostwriting because they want to rewrite everything. Founders who hate writing trust the process faster. The raw material we need is thinking and opinions — not writing ability. Some of our most effective founder content comes from executives who haven't written a LinkedIn post in two years.

Can we do authority positioning for a category that doesn't yet exist?

This is the highest-leverage case for authority positioning. If your company is defining a new category — a new way of thinking about a problem your buyers have — the founder is the only credible voice for that narrative. Category creation through founder content is a 12–18 month play, but companies that execute it successfully tend to own the category conversation before any competitor understands what happened.
