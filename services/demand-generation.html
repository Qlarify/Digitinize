03 / Demand Generation & ABM

Demand generation for teams with long, considered sales cycles.

Demand generation is not lead generation. Most demand gen programmes underperform because they're really lead gen programmes in a better wrapper. Our demand generation and ABM agency, based in Bangalore, India, builds the other kind: a system that creates pipeline from buyers at every stage of the journey — across both B2B and considered-purchase B2C — not just the ones who filled a form.

The Problem

Why most demand gen programs underperform.

The agency runs LinkedIn ads, gates a whitepaper, calls every form-fill an "MQL," and reports a cost-per-lead number that has no relationship to closed-won.

Most of your future buyers are not in-market today. Research consistently puts the in-market window at 5–10% of your addressable audience at any given time. Real demand generation builds a system that captures the other 90–95% — through search, social, content, and ABM — so you're the default when they finally move.

Demand gen vs lead gen

The real difference — and why it matters for pipeline.

The distinction isn't semantic. Lead generation optimises for form fills; demand generation optimises for revenue. The difference shows up in your pipeline data within 90 days.

LEAD GENERATION
  • →Gates content behind forms to capture contact details
  • →Optimises for cost-per-lead (a metric that doesn't predict revenue)
  • →Passes all form fills to sales as "MQLs"
  • →Sales team spends 70% of time on leads that will never close
  • →Scales by increasing budget, not by improving quality
DEMAND GENERATION
  • ✓Creates demand by educating buyers before they're in-market
  • ✓Optimises for pipeline velocity and closed-won revenue
  • ✓Only passes sales-ready signals to the sales team
  • ✓Sales team spends 90% of time on accounts with genuine intent
  • ✓Scales by improving ICP definition and channel mix — not just budget

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

How much does demand generation cost in India?

A meaningful demand gen program in India typically requires ₹6L–₹15L/month combined — covering paid media, content production, tooling (HubSpot or equivalent), and agency fees. The floor for seeing compounding results is ₹6L/month; below that you're running isolated campaigns, not a system. High-ACV enterprise programs (₹50L+ deal sizes) typically invest ₹15L–₹30L/month because the economics justify it — a single deal closed from demand gen justifies 6–12 months of spend.

What is ABM (account-based marketing) and how does it fit into demand gen?

ABM is demand generation focused on a named list of target accounts rather than a broad audience. Instead of casting a wide net, you run coordinated campaigns across LinkedIn, email, content, and sales outreach aimed at the specific 50–300 companies (in B2B) or high-value segments (in considered-purchase B2C) most likely to buy. ABM is a subset of demand generation — they use the same channels, but ABM adds account- or segment-level personalisation and a tighter coordination between marketing and sales. Most B2B companies with ACV above ₹20L — and B2C brands with LTVs that justify segment-level investment — benefit from running both: broad demand gen for awareness, ABM for high-value accounts or segments.

What's the difference between demand gen and ABM?

ABM is a subset of demand gen focused on named accounts (or tightly-defined segments). Most teams need both — in different ratios depending on ACV. High-ACV: ABM-heavy. Mid-market: balanced. PLG and high-volume B2C: demand-gen-heavy.

How many target accounts can we run ABM against?

50–200 typically. Beyond that, it's just segmented demand gen — the personalization breaks down and the economics stop working.

What's the minimum monthly investment?

₹6L–₹10L combined paid + content + tooling is the floor for meaningful demand gen — whether B2B or considered-purchase B2C — in India. Below that, you're running campaigns, not a program.

Do we need a marketing automation platform first?

Yes — HubSpot, Marketo, or similar. We help architect the setup if you don't have one. Without marketing automation, demand generation is a series of manual interventions. You can't scale sequencing, scoring, or attribution without the infrastructure.

How is this different from your LinkedIn service?

LinkedIn is a channel. Demand gen is the system that uses LinkedIn, SEO, content, and email in coordination. Most clients run both — LinkedIn becomes one input to the demand gen program.

How do you measure demand generation ROI?

We track three things: pipeline sourced (first-touch attribution), pipeline influenced (multi-touch), and pipeline velocity (how fast deals move through the funnel in accounts touched by demand gen). Most clients see a 20–35% improvement in pipeline velocity within 6 months of a properly instrumented demand gen program — often more valuable than the raw sourcing number.

We have a 3-person marketing team. Is demand gen realistic?

Yes — with the right architecture. A 3-person team trying to run five channels themselves will burn out; the same team with us as an embedded partner can run a full demand gen program because we handle production, distribution, and reporting. What we need from your team: a clear ICP, access to sales data, and a monthly 60-minute strategy call.

Can demand gen work for companies with a very narrow TAM?

It's actually where demand gen shines. If your total addressable market is 200–500 accounts, you can know every account by name, run personalised ABM sequences, and track engagement at the individual-account level. A narrow TAM isn't a demand gen obstacle — it's a demand gen advantage. We've run ABM programs for clients with fewer than 300 target accounts that outperform mass-market lead gen programs by 4–6x on pipeline per rupee.
