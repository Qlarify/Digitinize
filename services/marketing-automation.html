Marketing automation built for B2B revenue.

The Problem

Why most marketing automation programs underperform.

A year in, most of those platforms are running at a fraction of their capability — sequences never built, scoring never configured, attribution never connected to CRM.

Tools are bought before strategy is defined. Lead scoring is set up before anyone knows what a qualified lead actually looks like in closed-won data. Email sequences get built without a clear lifecycle map. Fix the strategy first; the tool is the easy part.

Automation maturity

Four stages of B2B automation maturity.

Most B2B companies are at Stage 1 or 2. The compounding returns — shorter sales cycles, better MQL-to-SQL conversion, predictable pipeline — happen at Stage 3 and 4. Here's how to get there.

STAGE 1

Basic outreach (welcome emails, one-off blasts)

Most companies start here. A welcome sequence, a monthly newsletter, and occasional one-off campaign emails. No scoring, no lifecycle logic, no CRM integration. The platform is underutilized and the team knows it.

STAGE 2

Triggered nurture (behaviour-based sequences)

Automation responds to actions: page visits, content downloads, demo requests. Sequences are triggered by behaviour, not just time. Lead scoring exists but is based on activity points, not predictive of close. Most HubSpot and Marketo installations sit here.

STAGE 3

Revenue-predictive scoring (closed-won model)

Lead scoring calibrated to closed-won data — not activity templates. Scores predict conversion probability, not just engagement. Sales-marketing handoff is automated with clear SLAs. Marketing knows which sequences are generating pipeline; sales knows which leads to call first.

STAGE 4

Full attribution loop (automation drives pipeline visibility)

Every marketing touchpoint is tracked from first awareness to closed-won. Marketing budget decisions are driven by pipeline data, not activity metrics. Automation is recalibrating continuously based on what's actually closing. This is where CAC starts dropping and pipeline velocity accelerates.

Platform comparison

HubSpot vs Marketo vs others — which platform is right for your stage?

Platform choice matters less than implementation quality. A well-implemented HubSpot outperforms a poorly configured Marketo every time. That said, the right platform depends on your team size, deal complexity, and CRM setup.

PLATFORM BEST FOR TYPICAL TEAM SIZE OUR RECOMMENDATION
HubSpot Series A–C B2B, up to ~500 contacts/month, sales + marketing alignment 2–20 marketing Default choice for most B2B under ₹100Cr ARR
Marketo Enterprise, complex scoring models, Salesforce-heavy stacks 5–50 marketing When Salesforce is the CRM and team has dedicated MOps
Customer.io Product-led B2B SaaS, event-based lifecycle, high volume 2–10 marketing + engineering When product data drives marketing (PLG motion)
ActiveCampaign SMB B2B, lower ACV, simpler lifecycle 1–5 marketing Budget-constrained teams not ready for HubSpot

We work in all four platforms. Our recommendation is always based on your stack and team — not our platform margin.

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

Which platforms do you work with?

HubSpot most often. Marketo, Salesforce Marketing Cloud, and Customer.io for specific cases. We pick based on your stack and team — not the platform we're best paid to recommend.

Do you migrate platforms?

Yes — typically from generic ESPs (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.) to HubSpot or Marketo. We've also done HubSpot-to-Marketo for enterprise stage clients.

Will you do the email writing?

Yes — all lifecycle and nurture content is produced in-house. Your team reviews and approves; you don't write.

How is this different from your demand gen service?

Demand gen brings buyers into the system. Automation accelerates them through it. Most clients run both — and the two services compound.

What's the typical timeline to a full automation buildout?

60–90 days to first stable state. ~6 months to full optimization with attribution and lead scoring calibrated to actual closed-won outcomes.

We already have HubSpot but it's barely used. Do we need to start over?

Rarely. A messy HubSpot is usually fixable without a full rebuild. We audit what exists — contact properties, workflows, sequences, deal pipeline — and salvage what's clean. Most under-used HubSpot installs can be operational within 30–45 days of a structured cleanup, compared to 60–90 days for a greenfield buildout.

How does marketing automation connect to our sales outreach?

In a well-built system, automation and sales outreach are two layers of the same motion. Marketing automation handles the pre-sales nurture (awareness, education, re-engagement). Sales sequences handle the post-intent outreach (follow-up, multi-threading, closing). The handoff logic — what triggers a lead going from marketing to sales — is the most important piece we design, because it's where most pipeline leaks.

What's the ROI case for marketing automation investment?

The most direct ROI is sales cycle compression. Companies with mature lifecycle automation typically see 25–40% shorter sales cycles on marketing-sourced leads, because buyers arrive at the sales conversation already educated. Secondary ROI: MQL quality improves (fewer low-intent leads going to sales), re-engagement rates improve (dormant leads that come back convert at 2–3x the rate of cold outreach), and marketing attribution becomes defensible in board conversations.
