07 / UI / UX Design

UI / UX design that respects the user — and the conversion rate.

Research-led design for marketing sites, dashboards, and product surfaces. User research, information architecture, wireframes, hi-fi design, prototype, design system, handoff. Built in Figma, shipped to engineering teams that can actually build it. Bangalore-based, working with B2B and product teams across India and globally.

The Problem

Most design work never gets shipped the way it was drawn.

A polished Figma file lands in engineering's lap. Spacing tokens are missing. States are undefined. Edge cases weren't considered. The build slowly diverges from the design, the designer gives up reviewing PRs, and what ships is a worse version of what was approved.

Design is engineering with a head start. We treat handoff as a first-class deliverable — design tokens, named components, defined states, written interaction specs — and most of our designs ship through engineering teams we also build with.

What we design

Four kinds of design work.

🧠

Product UX

Core product flows, onboarding, settings, billing, empty states. Research-led, instrumented for the metric the team is trying to move — activation, retention, expansion.

🌐

Marketing site design

Home, product, pricing, customer story templates. Designed for SEO structure and conversion — not just for the launch screenshot.

📊

Dashboard design

Data-dense interfaces, filtering, drill-downs, comparison views. The hard problem is hierarchy — what gets the foreground and what waits in a panel.

🧩

Design systems

Token libraries, component sets, documentation. Built so engineering can ship without asking for a redline, and so the next designer can extend it without breaking what's there.

Process

Research → IA → Wireframe → Hi-fi → Prototype → Handoff.

Each stage gets reviewed and signed off before the next starts. Scope doesn't drift after design is locked because the structural decisions were made before pixels were pushed.

RESEARCH

Understand the user and the job-to-be-done

User interviews, analytics review, competitive teardowns, internal stakeholder alignment. Output: a written brief with the user, the problem, the success metric, and the constraints.

IA

Information architecture and flows

Sitemap, user flows, content model. The structural decisions that determine whether the rest of the design will work — done before anyone opens Figma to push pixels.

WIREFRAME

Low-fi layout, fast iteration

Greyscale wireframes for the key surfaces. Hierarchy, layout, content priority. Cheap to change here, expensive later — most of the real decisions get made at this stage.

HI-FI

Visual design and all states

Full hi-fi designs in Figma. Every state — default, hover, active, focus, error, empty, loading. Tokens applied. Components named. Built so engineering can pull a component and ship it without inventing what's missing.

PROTOTYPE

Clickable flow for review and testing

Interactive prototype for stakeholder review and, where it makes sense, usability testing. Catches flow problems before engineering writes a line of code.

HANDOFF

Engineering-ready package

Annotated Figma files, design tokens exported, interaction specs written, walkthrough with the engineering lead. Designer stays on through the first sprint to review what ships against what was designed.

Outcomes

What good design looks like, measured.

✅

Higher task completion

Users finish the flow they came for. Drop-off in core funnels — signup, checkout, activation — measured against the pre-redesign baseline.

📈

Engagement on the surfaces that matter

Time-on-task in productive flows, return visits to the dashboard, feature adoption. Engagement that ties to retention — not vanity scrolls.

🤝

Lower handoff churn

Engineering ships what was designed. Fewer "design review" rounds, fewer back-and-forth tickets, fewer pixel-perfect debates. The cost of building goes down.

📐

A system the team can extend

Tokens, components, documentation. The next designer or engineer can ship a new surface without breaking what's there or asking how it's supposed to work.

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

What's your design process?

Research, information architecture, wireframes, hi-fi design, prototype, and handoff. Each stage gets reviewed and signed off before the next one starts so scope doesn't drift after pixels are locked. For smaller engagements we compress stages but never skip them — IA decisions made at the wireframe stage are 10x cheaper than catching them at hi-fi.

Do you do user research too?

Yes. User interviews, usability tests, and analytics review are part of every engagement where there are real users to talk to. For greenfield work — pre-product or pre-launch — we lean on competitive teardowns, proxy research with similar audiences, and internal stakeholder interviews.

How do you hand off to engineering?

Figma files with named components, design tokens exported (CSS variables or Tailwind config), interaction specs written for every non-obvious state, and a live walkthrough with the engineering lead. Where we also build the front-end, handoff is internal and tighter. Where engineering is in-house, we stay on for the first sprint to review PRs.

Can you work with our existing design system?

Yes — and we'd prefer to. If you have a design system that works, we use it, extend it where the new work requires, and document the gaps. We don't rebuild what already works just to put our name on it.

How much does UI/UX design cost in India?

Engagements typically run ₹2L–₹10L depending on scope. A single feature redesign or a marketing site lands on the lower end. A full product surface, dashboard suite, or design system build lands on the upper end. We'll scope precisely after a 30-minute call.

Can you do design and development in one engagement?

Yes. Our design and engineering teams sit together, and most projects move from Figma to ship inside one engagement. That removes the handoff churn that kills most agency design work.

What's the difference between UI and UX in your process?

UX is the structural work — research, IA, flows, wireframes. UI is the visual layer — typography, colour, components, motion. Most of what determines whether a product is good happens in UX. UI is what people notice first and judge fastest. Both matter; we don't separate them into different engagements.
