01 / SEO & Content

SEO built around long, considered buying cycles.

Most SEO programs are optimised for traffic. Yours should be optimised for pipeline. Our SEO agency, based in Bangalore, India, builds organic search around how considered-purchase buyers (B2B and B2C) actually search — over months, across multiple sessions, with research-stage queries that look nothing like the commercial queries you eventually want to win.

Book a diagnostic → See all services
The Problem

Why most SEO programs underperform.

Generalist SEO playbooks were built for high-volume, low-consideration searches. Considered purchases — whether B2B SaaS, IT services, or high-ticket B2C categories like financial products, education, or premium services — don't work that way. Your highest-value commercial keyword may have 90 searches a month, and ranking #1 for it may produce more pipeline than 50,000 monthly visits to a "what is X" blog post.

Most agencies optimise for traffic because traffic is easy to report. We optimise for the 200-search-a-month commercial terms your buyers use when they're ready to evaluate vendors — and the long-tail informational queries that bring them in 9 months earlier.

What we do

The four pillars of a working SEO program.

⚙️

Technical SEO foundation

Crawl, index, schema, and Core Web Vitals audit and remediation. The infrastructure that lets Google rank you at the moment your buyers are searching — and stops invisible drag from killing campaigns that should work.

🗺️

Topic cluster architecture

Topic clusters mapped to your ICP, deal size, and 6–18 month buying cycle. Every cluster has a commercial anchor and informational supports — and a clear conversion path back to your service or product page.

✍️

Editorial production

Four to eight in-depth pieces per month, founder-bylined where appropriate. Each piece carries a strong point of view, internal links to relevant clusters, and a clear next step for readers who are ready to engage.

📊

Content inventory & retirement

Quarterly content inventory: winners get expanded, losers get killed, gaps get filled. Most sites have 80% dead content dragging crawl budget. We fix that.

Who it works for

SEO for SaaS, IT services, consulting, and considered-purchase consumer brands in India.

Not every company is at the right stage for SEO investment. The B2B and B2C companies that get the most from an SEO program typically share a few traits: a defined ICP or customer segment, a buying cycle of 3 months or longer, and a product or service that buyers research before they evaluate options. That includes B2B SaaS and IT services, but also high-consideration B2C categories — financial products, education, premium home goods, healthcare — where buyers compare for weeks before they commit.

🏗️

B2B SaaS

Typically the best fit. Buyers research extensively before trials. Topic clusters around the problem your software solves — not the software itself — pull in buyers at every stage. Integration and comparison keywords ("HubSpot alternative", "best [category] tool for [vertical]") convert at high rates.

💻

IT Services & System Integrators

Strong fit for intent-based SEO. Buyers search for outcomes ("cloud migration for manufacturing", "SAP implementation partner India") not vendors. Ranking for outcome-level queries generates higher-quality leads than most paid programs.

📋

Management Consulting & Professional Services

Works for firms that have a point of view — not just credentials. The edge in consulting SEO is thought leadership that goes deeper than your competitors' whitepapers. Long-form, opinion-led content on practice areas your buyers research.

💰

Considered-Purchase B2C & Fintech

High-ticket consumer categories — financial products, insurance, education, premium services, healthcare — share the long-research dynamics of enterprise software. Buyers research for weeks or months, compare across sessions, and convert when the search returns content that answers a specific question. Longer timeline (9–15 months to pipeline impact) but compounding organic returns.

SEO vs Paid

When to prioritise SEO. When to start with paid.

Both channels work for considered purchases — B2B and B2C alike. The right starting point depends on your sales cycle, average deal size, and how much time you have before pipeline pressure becomes a board conversation.

START WITH SEO IF
  • ✓Sales cycle is 6 months or longer
  • ✓Average deal value is above ₹25L
  • ✓Your buyers do independent research before engaging vendors
  • ✓You have 12+ months before you need aggressive pipeline growth
  • ✓Competitors rank well for your category keywords
START WITH PAID IF
  • →You need pipeline in the next 90 days
  • →You're entering a new market or launching a new product
  • →Your ICP is very narrow (under 5,000 accounts)
  • →Search volume for your category is low or near zero
  • →You're pre-PMF and still testing messaging

Most companies at Series A and beyond should run both. SEO builds compounding equity; paid fills the gap while SEO matures. See how we run LinkedIn and paid media →

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

How much does B2B SEO cost in India?

SEO engagements for Indian companies — B2B or considered-purchase B2C — typically start at ₹1.5L–₹2.5L per month, covering 4–6 articles, monthly technical audit, internal linking, and topic strategy. Larger programs with digital PR, link earning, and content refresh cycles run ₹3L–₹6L/month. One-off technical audits start at ₹50K. The right investment depends on your competitive category, current organic baseline, and how fast you want to move — we scope this precisely after a diagnostic.

How long until we see results from SEO?

Material traffic shifts at 4–6 months. Pipeline impact at 8–12. We won't promise faster — anyone who does is either lying or about to publish 60 thin posts a month that Google will ignore.

Do you only work with companies that already have content?

No. Greenfield SEO is often easier than recovering a poorly-built site. We've taken clients across SaaS, IT services, and consulting from near-zero organic visibility to first-page positions on their primary category keywords — typically within 12–18 months.

Will you publish under our team's bylines or yours?

Yours. The byline strategy is part of authority positioning, and the credit belongs to your operators. We work from founder and SME interviews and edit toward your voice.

How do you measure SEO impact?

Search Console clicks and impressions for traffic, but pipeline attribution — first-touch and assisted — for impact. We build the attribution into your CRM during onboarding so the reporting reflects revenue, not vanity metrics.

What's the typical scope?

Six articles per month, quarterly technical audit, monthly internal linking pass, and topic strategy reviewed every 90 days. Larger programs add link earning, digital PR, and content refresh cycles.

What makes considered-purchase SEO different from impulse-purchase marketing?

Buyers in long-cycle purchases involve multiple people and multiple sessions. B2B buying committees are 3–7 people with different information needs — your CFO googles different things than your CTO. High-ticket B2C decisions (a home loan, an MBA, a luxury appliance) typically involve 2–4 household or stakeholder voices. SEO for these audiences builds content for each role, not a single buyer persona. It also operates on much longer attribution windows — organic search may be the first touch 12–18 months before a deal closes, which most analytics setups miss entirely.

Should we still invest in SEO if our sales team mainly uses outbound?

Yes — more so. Outbound opens the door; SEO closes it. When a prospect receives a cold email and googles your company, what they find determines whether the call happens. A strong organic presence makes outbound conversion rates significantly higher. In our experience, companies with active SEO programs see 20–35% higher outbound response rates within 12 months.

How do you handle competitor keyword targeting?

Carefully. We target competitor comparison queries ("X vs Y", "X alternative") because they convert — buyers using these terms are actively evaluating. We don't target competitor branded terms directly on Google, but we do build category content that positions you well in the comparison conversation before a buyer starts searching.
