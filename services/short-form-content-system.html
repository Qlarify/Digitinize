Services / Short-Form Content System

Short-form needs a system, not a shoot.

Most short-form content programmes plateau after month three. The shoots get expensive, the hooks stop landing, the editor goes through the motions. Short-form is a system problem — not a creative problem. Monthly studio day, hook library, editor-strategist pairing, and a distribution rhythm that compounds. Thirty, sixty, or ninety pieces a month from one shoot.

The problem

Why one-off shoots don't scale short-form.

The pattern: a brand commissions a shoot, gets twelve clips, posts them across six weeks, then runs out. Repeat in three months. The economics don't work and the audience never builds momentum.

⏱️

No production rhythm

Stop-start shoots, no shoot calendar, no editor continuity. The algorithm rewards weekly cadence; one-off shoots can't deliver it.

🎣

No hook library

Every video gets a fresh hook written from scratch. The hooks that worked last month don't get systematised — they get forgotten.

👥

Editor-as-vendor relationship

Freelancers cycle in and out. Style consistency collapses. The strategic insight that should pair with the edit never accumulates.

📉

No feedback loop

Vanity metrics (views, likes) tracked, but the hook-level performance data that should refresh the library never gets read.

Our model

Monthly studio + field, editor-strategist paired.

One studio day per month, supplemented by light field-shoot capture. An editor-strategist pair assigned to the channel. A hook library refined month-over-month. The whole thing engineered to compound.

🎬

Monthly studio day

One 4–6 hour controlled-light shoot. Multi-cam, prompted, designed to capture 30+ hook variants and 8–10 long-form blocks for derivative editing.

📱

Field shoot supplement

Light-crew field captures around events, meetings, offices, and locations. Adds context and texture to the studio core.

🤝

Editor-strategist pair

One editor and one strategist assigned per channel. They co-own hook selection, edit rhythm, and the performance feedback loop.

📚

Hook library

Hooks tagged, tested, and ranked. The library compounds quarterly — winning patterns scaled, dead patterns retired.

🎨

Brand template system

Captions, motion graphics, end cards, and on-brand visual layers built once, applied at edit time. Style consistency without per-piece overhead.

📊

Monthly performance read

Hook performance, retention curves, platform-specific signals. The data that decides what gets shot next month — not what felt good.

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

How many short-form pieces do you produce per month?

Volume depends on tier — 30 pieces per month on the entry tier, 60 on standard, 90+ on full. All cut from a single monthly studio day plus optional field-shoot supplement.

How do you keep style consistency across so many pieces?

An editor-strategist pair owns the channel. Same editor, same brand template, same hook library — refined month-over-month. Style is enforced by the system, not by review cycles.

How do hooks work?

Every shoot day captures a hook library — 20–40 hook variants per piece, designed against the audience and platform. The editor selects the strongest hook per cut. A/B testing on Meta and YouTube refreshes the library quarterly.

Do you A/B test hooks and edits?

Yes, on paid channels. Organic reach drives natural A/B testing; paid Meta and YouTube get structured creative tests where the hook library is statistically read for winners.

Is there a lock-in period?

Three-month minimum, monthly thereafter. The minimum protects production rhythm — month one is system setup, months two and three are output that compounds.

Can we add long-form to a short-form retainer?

Yes. Long-form add-ons (LinkedIn long-form, YouTube essays, podcast episodes) sit on top of the short-form retainer at a per-piece cost. Many clients run both as one system.
