Website development built for pipeline — not just pretty.

Marketing sites, product surfaces, and landing pages — engineered for SEO, speed, and conversion. We build on Webflow, Next.js, WordPress, and Astro, picking the stack your team can actually maintain after launch. Bangalore-based, working with B2B and product teams across India and globally.

The Problem

Most agency sites look fine and convert badly.

A new site ships, the team is proud of it, traffic flat-lines two months later. The page weight is too heavy for Core Web Vitals to recover. The CMS is built for the agency to update, not the marketing team. The pages were never wired into the SEO map or the campaign funnel.

A website is a piece of revenue infrastructure. Fast, indexable, easy to update, instrumented for conversion. We treat it that way from the first sketch.

Stack & approach

We pick the stack that fits your team.

There's no single right framework. The right one is the one your team can ship in after we leave. Most builds land on one of four stacks.

WEBFLOW

Marketing sites the team can maintain

Default for marketing sites under ~150 pages. Visual CMS, fast page load, structured content the team can update without engineering. Where most B2B marketing teams should be.

NEXT.JS

Product surfaces and engineering-owned sites

When the marketing site and the product overlap — pricing tied to live data, docs synced with the codebase, dashboards behind auth. Headless CMS, component library, full control.

WORDPRESS

Content-heavy, editorial sites

Still the right answer for content-first sites — blogs with hundreds of authors, editorial workflows, plugin ecosystems. We build headless WordPress for teams that want the CMS but not the theme.

ASTRO

Performance-first marketing sites

When Core Web Vitals are the constraint and the site is mostly static. Near-perfect Lighthouse scores out of the box, MDX content, partial hydration where it matters.

FAQ

Questions we get most often.

What stacks do you build on?

Webflow, Next.js, WordPress, and Astro most often. We pick based on what your team can maintain after we leave — not the framework we have the most reps in. If your team has strong engineers, Next.js is often right. If marketing owns the site day-to-day, Webflow usually wins.

How long does a typical website project take?

A marketing site rebuild typically runs 8–14 weeks from kickoff to launch — discovery and IA in weeks 1–3, design and content in weeks 3–8, build and QA in weeks 8–12, launch and stabilisation in weeks 12–14. Landing pages run 2–4 weeks. Product surfaces and complex builds are scoped separately.

Do you handle migrations?

Yes. The most common path is WordPress to Webflow, or static HTML to Next.js. Migrations include content audits, redirect mapping, SEO continuity checks, and a phased cutover plan so traffic doesn't drop in the weeks after launch.

How much does website development cost in India?

Marketing sites typically run ₹3L–₹15L depending on scope — page count, custom design depth, integrations, and CMS complexity drive the range. Landing pages run ₹50K–₹2L each. Migrations and product surfaces are scoped separately. We'll give you a fixed price after a 30-minute scoping call.

Will the marketing team be able to update the site themselves?

Yes. Every site ships with a structured CMS, named components, and a written handoff doc. The marketing team should be publishing new pages, swapping copy, and updating visuals on day one of post-launch — without filing a ticket with engineering or coming back to us.

Do you do design too, or do we need a separate designer?

We do both. UI/UX design and development sit on the same team. If you already have a designer or design system, we'll work to it. If you don't, we'll handle design as part of the build.

How do you handle SEO during a rebuild?

SEO is part of the build, not a phase after. We audit existing rankings and traffic, map old URLs to new, build schema and internal linking into the templates, and watch Search Console daily for the first 30 days post-launch. The goal is zero traffic loss through the cutover.
